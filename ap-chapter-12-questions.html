The most important part of the brain in terms of our immediate survival is the:

Brainstem

Explanation:

The brainstem controls the basic life-sustaining functions that keep you alive without conscious effort. These include:

Breathing

Heart rate

Blood pressure regulation

Swallowing

Reflexes like coughing and gagging

If the cerebrum is damaged, a person may lose consciousness or higher thinking, but they can still survive.

If the brainstem is damaged, those automatic survival functions stop — which is usually fatal.

So the brainstem is essential for immediate survival.
