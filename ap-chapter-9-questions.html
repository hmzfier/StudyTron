Match the word with the term

Circular – These muscles feature fascicles that are arranged around an opening, controlling the opening and closing of passageways in the body.

Parallel – These muscles feature fascicles that run parallel to the long axis, leading to a high range of motion but less force.

Fusiform – These muscles have a spindle-shaped design where the fascicles run parallel along the muscle but converge at the ends, allowing for a combination of strength and range of motion.

Pennate – These muscles feature fascicles that are attached obliquely to a central tendon, resembling a feather, resulting in higher force production but a limited range of motion.

Convergent – These muscles feature a broad origin with fascicles converging toward a single tendon of insertion leading to a versatile range of movements.
