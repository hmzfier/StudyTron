Define anatomy and physiology

Anatomy = structure

Physiology = function

the characteristics of living organisms

• Cells: the basic units of life

• Metabolism: Anabolism and Catabolism

• Growth

• Excretion

• Responsiveness or irritability

• Movement of – Entire organism, Individual cells within organism, Materials within or between cells of organism

• Reproduction - two forms in multicellular organisms: 1. Individual cells reproduce during growth and to replace damaged or old cells, 2. Organism itself reproduces; yields similar offspring

the direction terms and be able to apply them

Superior – above

Heart is superior to the stomach.

Inferior – below

Stomach is inferior to the heart.

Anterior (Ventral) – front

Sternum is anterior to the spine.

Posterior (Dorsal) – back

Spine is posterior to the sternum.

Medial – toward midline

Big toe is medial to the pinky toe.

Lateral – away from midline

Thumb is lateral to the pinky.

Proximal – closer to point of attachment (limbs)

Elbow is proximal to the wrist.

Distal – farther from point of attachment (limbs)

Fingers are distal to the elbow.

Superficial – toward the surface

Skin is superficial to muscle.

Deep – away from the surface

Bone is deep to the skin.

how to describe the anatomical position

Standing upright

Feet slightly apart

what homeostasis is

Homeostasis is the body’s ability to keep internal conditions stable (like temperature, pH, and blood sugar) despite changes outside.
