Lab Assessment 7A: Muscles

Muscles of facial Expression

Patterns

Shape: Frontalis is Parallel, Orbicularis oculi and oris are circular

Frontalis

Shape: Parallel

Action: Elevates Eyebrows, wrinkles skin on forehead horizontally

Origin: Epicranial Aponeurosis

Insertion: Skin of Eyebrows

Shape: Parallel

Orbicularis Oculi

Action: Closes eye

Origin: Frontal bone and maxilla

Insertion: Skin of the orbital area and eyelids

Shape: Circular

Orbicularis Oris

Action: Closes and protrudes lips

Origin: Maxilla and Mandible

Insertion: Lips

Shape: Circular

Eye

Patterns

Origin: All origin is posterior orbit except Inferior Oblique which has a Maxilla

Insertion: All insertion is Eye plus the same direction as the name. Example superior oblique has an insertion of eye superior. Exception:

SHAPE: All shapes are parallel except superior oblique is fusiform

superior oblique

Action: Pulls eye inferiorly and laterally

Origin: Posterior orbit

Insertion: Eye Superior/lateral

Shape: Fusiform

inferior oblique

Action: Moves eye superiorly and laterally

Origin: Maxilla

Insertion: Eye inferior

Shape: Parallel

inferior rectus

Action: Depresses Eye downward

Origin: Posterior orbit

Insertion: Eye Interior

Shape: Parallel

lateral rectus

action: Pulls eye laterally

Origin: Posterior orbit

Insertion: Eye lateral

Shape: Parallel

medial rectus

Action: Pulls eye medially

Origin: Posterior Orbit

Insertion: Eye Medial

Shape: Parallel

superior rectus

Action: Elevates eye upward

Origin: Posterior Orbit

Insertion: Eye Superior

Shape: Parallel

Mastication

Patterns

Insertion: Both temporalis and masseter are mandible

Masseter

Action: Elevates the Mandible

Origin: Zygomatic arch

Insertion: Mandible

Shape: Parallel

Temporalis

Action: Elevate and retracts the mandible

Origin: Temporal of the Skull

Insertion: Mandible

Shape: convergent

Muscles that move the head and Neck

Patterns:

ACTION (GENERAL) - Flexes, extends, or rotates

SHAPE: Scalene and Sternocleidomastoid are parallel, Trapezius is convergent

Scalene

Action: Flexes head laterally, Elevate rib cage, inspiration

Origin: C2-C7 Transverse Process

Insertion: First 2 ribs

Shape: Parallel

Sternocleidomastoid

Action: Flex and rotate head

Origin: Manubrium, Clavicle

Insertion: Temporal

Shape: Parallel

Trapezius (Superior)

Action: Extends the head (backward)

Origin: Occipital and Cervical Vertebrae

Insertion: Clavicle

Shape: convergent

Patterns

INSERTION: ALL scapula except Trapezius Upper is clavicle

Rhomboid major and minor

Action: Retract Scapula

Origin:

T2-T5 (major)

C7-T1 (minor)

pectoralis minor

serratus anterior

Trapezius

Muscles that Move the Arm

Patterns

SHAPE: all convergent except teres major which is fusiform

INSERTION: all 4 Humerus

ACTION: All of them adducts except deltoid which abducts.

ACTION (GENERAL) - Abducts, Adducts, Rotates Medially

All of them rotates medially the arm except deltoid.

Deltoid

Action: Abducts arm

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: Convergent

latissimus dorsi

Action: Adducts, Extends, Rotates Medially the arm

Origin: Iliac crest of pelvic, lumbar vertebrae

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: Convergent

pectoralis major

Action: Flexes, Adducts, rotates medially the arm

Origin: Clavicle, sternum, costal cartilages 1-7

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: convergent

teres major

Action: Adducts, Extends, Rotates Medially Arm

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: fusiform

Rotator cuff area

Patterns

ACTION: All 4 stabilize the shoulder joint (not the only action).

Infraspinatous and Teres minor both rotate laterally the humerus

ACTION (GENERAL) - Abducts, Adducts, Rotates Medially, Rotates Laterally, Stabilizes shoulder joint

ORIGIN: Brachialis, Brachioradalis, Triceps brachii are humerus.

Insertion: Brachialis and Triceps Brachii insertion is Ulna.

INSERTION: all 4 are Humerus

Infraspinatus

Action: Rotates Laterally the humerus, stabilizes shoulder joint

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: Convergent

subscapularis

Action: Adducts humerus, rotates medially humerus, stabilizes shoulder joint

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: Convergent

Supraspinatus

Action: Abduction Humerus, stabilizes shoulder joint

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: Parallel

teres minor

Action: Rotates Laterally Humerus, stabilizes shoulder joint

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Humerus

Shape: Fusiform

Muscles that move the forearm at the Elbow Joint

Patterns

Origin: Humerus

ACTION (GENERAL) - Flexes, Extends, Supinate

SHAPE: All of them are fusiform

biceps brachii

Action: Supinates, Flexes the forearm

Origin: Scapula

Insertion: Radial Tuberosity of Radius

Shape: Fusiform

Brachialis

Action: Flexes the forearm (prime mover)

Origin: Humerus

Insertion: Ulna

Shape: Fusiform

Brachioradialis

Action: Flexes the forearm (synergist)

Origin: Humerus

Insertion: Radius

Shape: Fusiform

triceps brachii

Action: Extends the forearm

Origin: Humerus, Scapula

Insertion: Ulna

Shape: Fusiform
