How is acid-based homeostasis maintained in the skeletal system bones?

What is the inorganic matrix of bone made of?

What are osteocytes and how do they form?

They are mature osteoblasts that become trapped in the ECM they secreted, maintaining bone tissue.

4 Steps of Intramembranous Ossification

1. Osteoblasts develop from messenchymal cells

2. Trapped osteoblasts become osteocytes

3. Osteoblasts create trabelculae of spongy bone. Periosteum is created

4. Osteoblasts in the periosteum create compact bone

5 steps of Endochondral Ossification

1. Perichondrium is invaded by blood vessels.

2. Perichondrial cells differentiate into osteogenic cells then into osteoblasts.

3. Osteoblasts build the bone collar on the external surface of the bone.

4. Chondrocytes die

5. In the primary ossification center, osteoblasts replace calcified cartilage with early spongy bone.

What are the 5 zones of endochondral ossification

The five zones of endochondral ossification (in a growing long bone’s epiphyseal plate) in order from epiphysis to diaphysis are:

1. Zone of Resting (Reserve) Cartilage – small, inactive chondrocytes that anchor the epiphyseal plate to the epiphysis.

2. Zone of Proliferation – chondrocytes divide rapidly, forming columns of stacked cells.

3. Zone of Hypertrophy – chondrocytes enlarge and mature.

4. Zone of Calcification – the cartilage matrix calcifies, chondrocytes begin to die.

5. Zone of Ossification – osteoblasts invade, lay down bone matrix, forming new bone.

This sequence is essential for longitudinal bone growth.

How do bones grow in width?

Through appositional growth, where osteoblasts in the periosteum secrete new circumferential lamellae.
