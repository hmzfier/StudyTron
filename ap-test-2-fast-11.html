Chapter 10

What is the electrical gradient or potential across the sarcolemma called?

The membrane potential—an electrical difference across the muscle cell’s plasma membrane.

Where are sodium and potassium ions most concentrated?

Sodium (Na⁺) concentration is higher in the extracellular fluid, while potassium (K⁺) concentration is higher in the cytosol.

What 2 things causes the negative resting membrane potential?

The continuous loss of potassium through leak channels and the activity of Na⁺/K⁺ pumps.

What 2 things do action potentials do

Depolarization, Repolarization

What is a single contraction-relaxation cycle of a muscle fiber called?

A twitch

What is recruitment in muscle contraction?

The activation of additional motor units to produce more forceful contractions.

What is muscle tone?

Small, involuntary contractions of alternating motor units that maintain posture and readiness.

What 4 things stimulates smooth muscle contraction?

The autonomic nervous system, stretch, hormones, and pacemaker cell activity.

What is the functional contractile unit of the myofibril?

Sarcomere

The main immediate source of ATP as muscle contractions begin comes from __________.

Creatine phosphate
