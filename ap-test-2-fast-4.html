The bone markings definitions

Depressions – pathways for blood vessels and nerves, or sites for articulations or joints between bones

Openings – enclose delicate structures; allow them to travel through bones

Projections – sites where bones articulate or where ligaments and tendons attach

Name 2 Primary curvatures, 2 secondary curvatures, 3 Abnormal Curvatures

Primary Curvatures (Present at Birth):

Thoracic curvature – convex posteriorly (kyphotic)

Sacral curvature – convex posteriorly (kyphotic)

Secondary Curvatures (Develop After Birth):

Cervical curvature – concave posteriorly - allow us to hold our heads up

Lumbar curvature – shifts weight of body onto sacrum  Lends balance and support needed to walk on two legs;

Abnormal Curvatures:

Kyphosis – exaggerated thoracic curve (“hunchback”)

Lordosis –exaggerated cervical and lumbar curvatures (“swayback”)

Scoliosis – lateral curvature of the spine

Compare and contrast the cervical, thoracic and lumbar vertebrae

Cervical

Body Shape = Oval

Vertebral Foramen Shape = Triangle

Transverse Process = Transverse Foramina

Spinous Process = 1 spike

Thoracic

Body Shape = larger and heart shapes

Vertebral Foramen Shape = Circle

Transverse Process - Long, has articular facets for ribs

Spinous Process = 3 spikes

Lumbar

Body Shape = largest and kidney shaped

Vertebral Foramen Shape = flattened triangular

Transverse Process = short
