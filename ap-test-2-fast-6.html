The 3 function of joints

Movement and stability and growth of bones

Synarthroses, Amphiarthroses, Diarthroses

Synarthroses: Immovable joints (e.g., sutures of the skull)

Amphiarthroses: Slightly movable joints (e.g., intervertebral discs, pubic symphysis)

Diarthroses: Freely movable joints (e.g., shoulder, hip)

What are fibrous joints made of?

Dense regular connective tissue

What do synovial joints have on the surface?

Hyaline cartilage

What covers the end of synovial joints?

Articular Cartilage

What 4 things do synovial joints have and their functions

1. Joint cavity (synovial cavity) – space between articulating bones

2. Articular capsule: Has two layers: Outer fibrous layer – holds the bones together Inner synovial membrane – lines the inside (except over cartilage) and produces synovial fluid

3. Synovial fluid – thick liquid with 3 main functions

Lubrication – reduces friction between articulating surfaces

Transportation – moves nutrients and waste products in absence of blood vessels within joint

Shock absorption – helps to evenly distribute stress and force during movement

4. Articular Cartilage:

Covers the ends of bones, reduces friction and absorbs shock

What is muscle tone

the slight, constant contraction of muscles even when you’re not actively moving.

Muscle tone is what keeps the bones of a synovial joint stable and properly aligned.
