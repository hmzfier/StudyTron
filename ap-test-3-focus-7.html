28. the 2 broad types of hearing loss and what structures are involved with each.

Conduction hearing loss – due to problem in outer or middle ear

• Prevents sound waves from reaching inner ear

• Causes: excessive buildup of ear wax, middle ear infection, perforated tympanic membrane, or fusion of auditory ossicles

Sensorineural hearing loss – deficit in cochlea or any part of neural pathway

1. Sensory hearing loss – action potentials cannot be generated in cochlea; usually due to hair cell dysfunction

• Exposure to loud sounds or certain medications may damage hair cells; also lose stereocilia as we age,

especially those in proximal part of cochlea (responsible for detecting high-frequency sounds)

• Hearing aids can help mild to moderate cases of sensory hearing loss

• cochlear implants may also be an option

2. Neural hearing loss – signals fail to travel through cochlear branch of vestibulocochlear nerve or CNS pathways

• Typical causes include strokes or tumors

29. what vestibular sensation is and the 2 types of equilibrium

Vestibular sensation: Sense of equilibrium: Sense of balance, head position, and head movement.

Two types of equilibrium:

Static equilibrium – maintaining balance when head is tilted but head and body are not moving

Dynamic equilibrium – maintaining balance when head or body is undergoing rotational or

angular motions (such as spinning in rotating chair) or linear acceleration (such as riding in car)
