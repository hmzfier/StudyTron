the location and function of the limbic system

Location: A group of 4 structures (fornix, limbic lobe (region of medial cerebrum), hippocampus, amygdala, and pathways) deep in the cerebrum around the diencephalon.

Function: Involved in memory, learning, emotion, and behavior

Pineal Gland function

Produces melatonin and regulates sleep–wake cycles.

Broca’s area

produces speech.

If damaged: you know what you want to say but can’t form words (non-fluent speech).

Wernicke’s area

understands language.

If damaged: you can speak fluently but the words don’t make sense and you can’t understand language.

what a special sense is versus a general sense

Special senses

Detected by specialized organs

include vision, hearing, taste, smell, and balance.

General senses

Detected by receptors throughout the body

include touch, pressure, pain, temperature, and proprioception.

Anterior root has what neurons

motor neurons from anterior horn (efferent)

Posterior root has what neurons

sensory neurons from posterior horn (afferent)

Describe a nerve plexus

a network of mixed spinal nerve branches that join and form new nerves for a body region.

Muscle spindles

detect muscle stretch; cause reflex contraction to prevent overstretching.

Golgi tendon organs

detect tendon tension; cause reflex relaxation to prevent excessive force.

where are preganglionic and postganglionic neurons located in the sympathetic nervous system

Preganglionic cell bodies location: Thoracolumbar region (T1–L2) of the spinal cord

Postganglionic cell bodies location: Sympathetic chain ganglia

Cholinergic receptors

Acetylcholine, Muscarinic, Nicotinic

Parasympathetic Neuron Location

Preganglionic cell bodies: Craniosacral region → brainstem nuclei (CN III, VII, IX, X) + S2–S4 spinal cord

Postganglionic cell bodies: located in or very near target organs

how the lens changes shape

Ciliary body surrounding lens relaxes when viewing distant objects

• Flattens lens, reducing its refractive capability

Ciliary body contracts when viewing nearby object; allows for accommodation

• Thickens lens, increasing refractive power
