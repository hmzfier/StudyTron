Which 2 organelles are not membrane bound?

Ribosomes and centrosomes

Mitochondria

Structure: Bean-shaped, double membrane

Function: Powerhouse of the cell - Make ATP (energy)

Ribosomes

Structure: Small protein/RNA particles (free or on rough ER)

Function: Site of Protein synthesis

Rough ER

Structure: Membranes covered in ribosomes

Function: Makes proteins for export

Smooth ER

Structure: Membrane network, no ribosomes

Function: Makes lipids, detoxifies drugs

Golgi Apparatus

Structure: Stacked, flat membranes

Function: Packages, modifies, and ships proteins

Lysosomes

Structure: Membrane sac of enzymes

Function: Digests waste, old organelles, bacteria

Peroxisomes

Structure: Enzyme-filled sacs

Function: Break down toxins, fatty acids

Function from powerpoint: use oxygen to oxidize organic molecules; produce hydrogen peroxide (H2O2)

Cytoskeleton - Important!

Structure: Protein fibers (microtubules, microfilaments)

Function: Shape, support, movement inside cell

Centrioles

Structure: Cylinders of microtubules

Function: Help with cell division (spindle fibers)

Vesicles

Structure: Small membrane bubbles

Function: Transport materials around the cell

Nucleus (technically its own component, but often grouped)

Structure: Membrane-bound; contains DNA

Function: Control center of the cell
