Four Types of CT Proper

1. Loose (Areolar) CT

Location: Beneath epithelium of skin, in membranes lining body cavities, and within walls of hollow organs

Function: Contains and supports blood vessels vital to avascular epithelial tissues; houses immune system cells

2. Dense Regular CT

Location: Tendons, ligaments

Function: Strong in one direction

3. Dense Irregular CT

Location: In high tension areas such as dermis (deep to skin) and surrounding organs and joints

Function: Strength in many directions

4. Adipose Tissue

Location: Under skin, around organs

Function: Energy storage, insulation, protection

SPECIALIZED CONNECTIVE TISSUE

1. Cartilage

Structure: Chondrocytes in lacunae; firm ECM

Cells: Chondroblasts – immature cells; divide by mitosis and make most of ECM

Location: Nose, trachea, joints

Function: Support + cushioning

Types: Hyaline, elastic, fibrocartilage

Hyaline cartilage – most abundant cartilage

ECM mostly ground substance made of small bundles of fine collagen; gives tissue glossy bluish-gray appearance

On ends of bones in joints (Articular cartilage), linking sternum to ribs, framing sections of respiratory tract, and in nose

Most of fetal skeleton is hyaline cartilage; replaced with bone during development

Fibrocartilage – filled with bundles of collagen fibers; little room for ground substance in ECM

Fibroblasts reside in tissue; also chondroblasts and chondrocytes; fill ECM with collagen and some elastic fibers

Tissue has great tensile strength with some degree of elasticity

In between bones of fibrous joints; forms articular discs that improve fit of bones in joints

Elastic cartilage

Mostly elastic fibers in ECM

Allows tissue to vibrate

In limited number of structures; external ear assists with detection of sound in air; larynx assists with production of sound

2. Bone

Structure: Osteocytes in lacunae; mineralized ECM

Location: Skeleton

Function: Support, protection, mineral storage

Osteoblasts : “Bone-builders” on outer surface of bones; closely associated with dense irregular

collagenous connective tissue covering (periosteum)

Osteocytes – osteoblasts that have surrounded themselves with ECM in lacunae; mostly

inactive; continue to make and secrete substances important for bone maintenance

Osteoclasts – bone destroyers; carry out process of bone resorption; secrete hydrogen ions

and enzymes that break down both inorganic and organic ECM

3. Blood

Structure: Plasma + cells (RBCs, WBCs, platelets)

Location: Vessels

Function: Transport gases/nutrients; immunity; clotting

Erythrocytes (red blood cells) bind to and transport oxygen throughout body

Leukocytes (white blood cells) function in immunity

Platelets – cell fragments; major role in blood clotting

Muscle tissues Basic Structure

Myocyte: main component of muscle tissue is muscle cell; excitable (respond toelectrical or chemical stimulation)

Muscle cells = fibers

Contain actin + myosin for contraction

Two forms of muscle cells based on arrangement of myofilaments (protein bundles) in cytoplasm:

•Striated – myofilaments arranged in alternating light and dark regions; appear striped (striated) under microscope

•Smooth – myofilaments arranged in irregular bundles instead of repeating light and dark regions

Endomysium – small amount of ECM that surrounds muscle tissue; helps hold muscle cells together in tissue

Muscle tissues Basic Functions

Movement

Posture

Heat production

3 types of muscle tissues

1. Skeletal Muscle

Structure: Striated, long, multinucleated

Location: Attached to bones

Function: Voluntary body movement

2. Cardiac Muscle

Structure: Striated, branched, 1 nucleus, intercalated discs

Location: Heart

Function: Pumps blood; involuntary

3. Smooth Muscle

Structure: No striations, spindle-shaped cells

Location: Hollow organs (stomach, intestines, vessels)

Function: Moves substances through organs; involuntary
