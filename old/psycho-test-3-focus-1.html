Intoxication

Reversible symptoms due to the direct effects of a substance ingested or exposed

Tolerance

Using the same amount of a substance no longer has the desired effect

Delirium tremens (DTs)

A severe form of alcohol withdrawal with confusion, agitation, hallucinations, fever, and autonomic instability. It’s a medical emergency and can be fatal without treatment.

Korsakoff’s syndrome

Korsakoff’s syndrome is a chronic memory disorder caused by long-term alcohol misuse.

It’s associated with vitamin B1 (thiamine) deficiency.

Confabulation

Confabulation is when a person fills in memory gaps with made-up stories without realizing they’re inaccurate.

It’s common in Korsakoff’s syndrome.

Harm Reduction

Reduce the negative effects of drug use

May include free condoms, needle exchange programs, Narcan distribution programs, “wet shelters”

fetal alcohol syndrome

Fetal alcohol syndrome is the pattern of birth defects caused by alcohol exposure in the womb.

It includes:

Growth problems

Smooth philtrum / thin upper lip

Small eye openings

Cognitive and behavioral deficits

Aversion therapy:

Pairing the substance (or substance-related cues) with something unpleasant to create a negative association. Example: Shock and alcohol, alcohol use while on drug that makes you vomit

Contingency management:

Giving rewards (vouchers, prizes, money equivalents) for clean drug tests or other sober behaviors. Typically not used in isolation (used with other treatments

Relapse prevention training:

Teaching patients to identify triggers, avoid high-risk situations, and use coping skills to prevent relapse.

Abstinence violation effect:

When a person slips once, then feels guilt/shame and thinks “I already messed up, so it doesn’t matter,” which leads to a full relapse.

Seemingly irrelevant decisions:

Small choices that don’t look risky on the surface but move someone closer to a high-risk situation (example: driving near an old hangout), increasing relapse chances.

Detoxification

Medically supervised withdrawal

Antagonist medications

Medications that block or change the effects of the drug of choice

Agonist medications

Mimic the effects of the drug of choice, used as a safer substitute for the drug of choice

Schizophrenia

Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech, disorganized or bizarre behavior.

being controlled by others; persecution (most common); reference; grandeur

Loose associations

thoughts that jump from topic to topic with little connection.

“The problem is insects. My brother used to collect insects. He’s now a man 5 foot 10. You know, 10 is my favorite number; I also like to dance, draw, and watch TV.”

Neologisms

made-up words

“This desk is a cramstile”; “He’s an easterhorned head”

Clang/clang association

when a person’s speech is driven by the sound of words (rhyming, alliteration) rather than meaning. They choose words because they sound alike, not because they make logical sense.

How are you? “Well, hell, it’s well to tell”

How’s the weather? “So hot, you know it runs on a cot”

Perseverance

Stay on a topic, repeat topic and/or words

Heightened perceptions

Feel that their senses are being flooded by sights and sounds, making it hard to attend to information and recall/remember information

Incongruent affect

displaying emotions that are not suited to the situation

Positive/Negative symptoms (schizophrenia)

positive = things that are added to normal behavior (extra behaviors that shouldn’t be there).

Negative symptoms = symptoms that take away normal abilities.

Alogia

Poverty of speech

Long lapses before responding or failure to answer

Reduction in quantity and speed of speech (convey little meaning)

Restricted affect /Blunted affect

reduced but not absent emotional expression.

Avoidance of eye contact

Immobile, expressionless face

Lack of emotion perceived by others

Monotonous voice, low and difficult to hear

Flat affect

no emotional expression (voice, face, gestures appear emotionless).

Avolition

Amotivation/apathy

Lack of energy, few interests, loss of motivation

Social withdrawal

Breakdown of social skills, often after many years with disorder

Difficulties recognizing others’ needs and emotions

Echolalia

Repeating someone else’s words or phrases.

Echopraxia

Imitating someone else’s movements or gestures.

Negativism

Resisting instructions or doing the opposite of what is asked, without an obvious reason.

Delusions

faulty interpretations of reality

Derailment

speech that drifts off track so ideas don’t follow logically.

Psychosis

loss of contact with reality, involving hallucinations, delusions, or disorganized thinking.

Premorbid functioning

How well a person was functioning before the onset of schizophrenia (socially, academically, emotionally, behaviorally).

Catatonia

A movement/behavioral syndrome where a person shows abnormal motor activity, decreased responsiveness, or excessive, purposeless movement.

Catatonic stupor

Little or no movement; unresponsive; appears “frozen.”

Catatonic rigidity

Maintaining a rigid, upright posture and resisting efforts to be moved.

Catatonic posturing

Holding an unusual or awkward position for long periods.

Catatonic excitement

Excessive, purposeless, and sometimes agitated movement.

Postpartum psychosis

a rare, severe psychiatric episode that happens within days to weeks after childbirth and involves:

hallucinations, delusions, severe confusion, disorganized behavior, rapid mood shifts, loss of touch with reality. It is a psychiatric emergency and requires immediate treatment.

schizophrenogenic mothers

A now-discredited idea that schizophrenia is caused by a mother who is cold, rejecting, controlling, and contradictory, creating confusion and emotional conflict in the child.

Research support: None.

This theory has been completely rejected—it is not supported by evidence and is considered inaccurate and harmful.

Neuroleptic drugs

Another name for conventional (first-generation) antipsychotics. They work mainly by blocking dopamine (D2) receptors.

Extrapyramidal effects (EPS)

Muscle tremor and rigidity

Tardive dyskinesia

Late-appearing, involuntary movements (lip smacking, tongue flicking, facial movements). Can be permanent.

Dystonia

unusual movements of the face, neck, tongue,and back

Akathisia

limb restlessness, agitation, discomfort

Agranulocytosis (a side effect of antipsychotics)

A serious side effect where the white blood cell count drops dangerously low (1–1.5% of patients).

Expressed emotion

A measure of family members’ attitudes toward a person with a mental disorder, including:

Criticism

Hostility

Emotional overinvolvement

Relation to schizophrenia:

High EE in families is linked to a higher risk of relapse in people with schizophrenia.

Families with low EE tend to support better outcomes.

Antisocial personality disorder

• Pervasive pattern of disregard for and violation of the rights of others occurring before the age of 15.

• Requires 3 or more of the following

• Failure to conform to social norms (repeatedly engages in unlawful behavior that would be grounds for arrest)

• Deceitful, hx of repeated lying, conning, use of aliases

• Impulsivity, failure to plan ahead

• Aggressiveness and irritability, hx of physical fights, assaults

• Reckless disregard for the safely of self or others

• Consistent irresponsibility (work, financial obligations)

• Lack of remorse for what they have inflicted on others

Evidence of conduct disorder onset b/f age 15

Borderline personality disorder

A pervasive pattern of instability in relationships, self image, emotions often with marked impulsivity beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts.

• Must have at least 5 of the following

• Frantic efforts to avoid real or imagined abandonment

• Pattern of unstable interpersonal relationships; vacillate between idealization and devaluation

• Identity disturbance; unstable self image, sense of self

• Impulsivity in at least 2 potentially dangerous areas

• Recurrent suicidal behavior, gestures, threats, self mutilation

• Unstable emotions and moods

• Chronic feelings of emptiness

• Inappropriate, intense anger, difficulties controlling anger

• Transient stress related paranoia or severe dissociation

Suicide - 70% attempt suicide at least once. 6% actually die

Risk of suicide peak during young adulthood and then gradually wane with advancing age

Mentalization (for borderline personality disorder)?

Difficulties understanding own and others’ mental and emotional states

Childhood abuse: In support of this theory, research has found that the parents of many people with borderline personality disorder did indeed neglect or reject them during their childhood, verbally abuse them, or otherwise behave inappropriately (Armour et al., 2022). The childhoods were often marked by multiple parent substitutes, divorce, death, or traumas such as physical or sexual abuse (Chapman, Jamil, & Fleisher, 2023; Yuan et al., 2023).

dialectical behavior therapy

Dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) is a structured form of therapy that teaches skills to help people manage intense emotions, reduce harmful behaviors, and improve relationships. It combines behavior therapy with mindfulness and emphasizes balancing acceptance with change.

The 4 DBT target areas for BPD:

1. Mindfulness

2. Tolerating distress

3. Emotion regulation

4. Interpersonal effectiveness

Alexithymia

Difficulty identifying, describing, and understanding one’s own emotions.

Narcissistic personality disorder

Must have 5 or more of the following

• Has a grandiose sense of self importance

• Is preoccupied with fantasies of unlimited success, power, beauty, brilliance

• Believes he/she is special and can only be understood by other special people of high status

• Requires excessive admiration

• Has a sense of entitlement

• Is interpersonally exploitive

• Lacks empathy

• Is often envious of others and believes others are envious of him/her

• Shows arrogant, haughty behaviors and attitudes

Avoidant personality disorder

A pervasive pattern of social inhibition, feelings of inadequacy, and hypersensitivity to negative evaluation, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment. Must have 4 or more sxs

• Avoids occupational activities involving significant interpersonal contact

• Unwilling to get involved w/others unless certain that they will be liked

• Shows restraint in relationships b/c of fear of being shamed or ridiculed

• Preoccupied with being criticized or rejected in social situations

• Inhibited in new interpersonal situations b/c of feelings of inadequacy

• Views self as socially inept, personally unappealing or inferior

• Usually reluctant to take personal risks or engage in new activities because they may prove embarrassing

Dependent personality disorder

A pervasive and excessive need to be taken care of that leads to submissive and clinging behavior and fears of separation, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment. Must have 5 or more:

• Difficulty making everyday decisions w/o excessive advice and/or reassurance

• Needs others to assume responsibility for most areas of his/her life

• Has difficulty disagreeing with others due to fear of losing support or approval

• Difficulty initiating projects alone due to low self confidence

• Goes to excessive lengths to obtain nurturance and support of others, may volunteer to do unpleasant things in order to be liked

• Uncomfortable/helpless when alone due to fears of being unable to care for self

• Urgently seeks another relationship if one ends

• Unrealistically preoccupied with fear of being left to care for self

Obsessive Compulsive Personality Disorder

A pervasive pattern of preoccupation with orderliness, perfectionism, and mental and interpersonal control, at the expense of flexibility, openness, and efficiency, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment.

• Must have 4 or more of the following

• Preoccupation with rules, details, lists, schedules, and organization to the point that the reason for an activity is lost

• Perfectionism that interferes with task completion

• Excessively devoted to work and productivity to the exclusion of leisure activities and friendships

• Overly conscientious and inflexible with regards to morals, ethics, values

• Unable to discard worn and/or worthless objects that have no sentimental value

• Reluctant to delegate tasks unless others submit to their exact way of doing things

• Miserly spending style (money viewed as something to be hoarded for future catastrophes)

• Rigidity and stubbornness

Histrionic Personality Disorder

A pervasive pattern of excessive emotionality and attention seeking, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment. Must have 5 or more:

• Uncomfortable in situations where they are not the center of attention

• Sexual seductive or provocative behavior

• Displays rapidly shifting, shallow expressions of emotion

• Consistently uses physical appearance to draw attention to self

• Speech is expressionistic and lacks detail

• Exaggerated, theatrical expressions of emotion

• High suggestible, easily influenced by others

• Considers relationships to be more intimate that they actually are

Paranoid personality disorder

A pervasive distrust and suspiciousness of others such that their motives are interpreted as malevolent, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment.

• Must have 4 or more of the following

• Suspects, without sufficient basis, that others are exploiting, harming or deceiving him or her

• Preoccupied with unjustified doubts about loyalty or trustworthiness of friends and associates

• Reluctant to confide in others due to fear that information will be used against him/her

• Reads hidden demeaning or threatening meanings into benign remarks or events

• Persistently bears grudges

• Perceives attacks on one’s character or reputation that others do not see and quick to react angrily or to counterattack

• Recurrent suspicions without justification of sexual partner’s fidelity

Schizoid personality disorder

A pervasive pattern of detachment from social relationships and a restricted range of expression of emotion in interpersonal settings, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment. Must have 4 or more of the following:

• Neither desires nor enjoys close relationships, including family

• Almost always chooses solitary activities

• Has little, if any, interest in sexual experiences with another person

• Takes pleasure in few activities

• Lacks close friends, confidants (exception: 1st degree relatives)

• Appears indifferent to praise or criticism

• Show emotional coldness, detachment or flat affect

•R/O schizophrenia, bipolar, MDD with psychotic features episode

Schizotypal personality disorder

A pervasive pattern of social and interpersonal deficits marked by acute discomfort with, and reducted capacity for, close relationships as well as cognitive or perceptual distortions and eccentricities of behavior, beginning in early adulthood. Present in a variety of contexts. Causes distress/impairment. Must have 5 or more:

• Ideas of reference (except delusions of reference)

• Odd beliefs and magical thinking that influence behavior but are inconsistent with subculture norms (superstitiousness, clairvoyance, telepathy, 6th sense)

• Unusual perceptions or paranoid ideation (including bodily illusions)

• Odd thinking and speech (i.e.,vague, circumstantial, over elaborate)

• Suspiciousness or paranoid ideation

• Inappropriate or constricted affect

• Behavior and/or appearance that is odd or eccentric

• Lacks close friends or confidents (besides 1st degree relatives)

• Excessive social anxiety that does not diminish with familiarity, associated with paranoid fear rather than negative judgment of self

•rule out schizophrenia, bipolar, MDD with psychotic features episode

Dark triad

A group of three socially aversive personality traits:

Narcissism – grandiosity, entitlement, need for admiration

Machiavellianism – manipulation, deceit, strategic exploitation

Psychopathy – callousness, impulsivity, lack of empathy or remorse
