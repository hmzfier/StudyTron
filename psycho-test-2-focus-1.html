Core Definitions

PTSD

A disorder that develops after experiencing or witnessing trauma, causing intrusive memories, avoidance, negative mood, and heightened arousal.

Acute Stress Disorder

Similar to PTSD but occurs within 3 days to 1 month after a traumatic event.

Dissociative Amnesia

Memory loss for personal information, usually related to trauma or stress, that cannot be explained by ordinary forgetfulness.

Dissociative Identity Disorder

Presence of two or more distinct identities that control behavior at different times, often with memory gaps.

Depersonalization-Derealization Disorder

Persistent and recurrent episodes of either or both: Depersonalization (detached from one's own body) or Derealization (detached from reality)

Prolonged Grief Disorder

Persistent, intense grief lasting more than a year, interfering with daily functioning after the loss of a loved one.

Unipolar Depression / Major depressive Disorder

Persistent low mood, loss of interest, and other symptoms that impair daily life for at least two weeks.

Persistent depressive disorder

Consistent depressed mood for at least two years

1 year for children

Premenstrual dysphoric disorder

Severe emotional and physical symptoms occurring before menstruation that disrupt daily functioning.

Post-Partum Depression

Depression that occurs within the first year after childbirth.

Bipolar 1 Disorder

Mood disorder with at least one manic episode, often with depressive episodes.

Bipolar 2 Disorder

Mood disorder with hypomanic episodes and major depressive episodes, but no full manic episodes.

Cyclothymic Disorder

Chronic mood instability with mild hypomanic and depressive symptoms that do not meet full criteria for bipolar disorder.

Mania

State of euphoria or frenzied energy

Anorexia

Eating disorder characterized by restriction of food intake, intense fear of gaining weight, and distorted body image.

Bulimia

Eating disorder involving binge eating followed by compensatory behaviors like vomiting, fasting, or excessive exercise.

Binge Eating Disorder

Eating disorder with recurrent binge eating episodes without compensatory behaviors, often causing distress or health problems.

Alexithymia

Condition whereby a person has a difficult time identifying feelings and putting feelings into words

This is common among people with anorexia and bulimia

What are the 2 pathways associated with fear?

Fast pathway: Thalamus → Amygdala (immediate reaction)

Slow pathway: Thalamus → Cortex → Amygdala (more processed, thoughtful response)

What is the HPA pathway?

The HPA pathway involves: Hypothalamus → Pituitary → Adrenal glands, resulting in cortisol release during stress.

What hormones are thought to be involved in PTSD?

Cortisol, norepinephrine, and sometimes reduced hippocampal volume.

How Are Eating Disorders Treated?

Eating disorder treatments have two main goals:

1.Correct dangerous eating patterns

2. Address broader psychological and situational factors that have led to, and are maintaining, the eating problem
