Beck's 4 cognitive Views on why depression occurs

1. Maladaptive attitudes = Self-defeating attitudes

2.Cognitive triad - Individuals repeatedly interpret (1) their experiences, (2) themselves, and (3) their futures in negative ways,leading to depression

3. Negative Thinking - Minimization of the positive and magnification of the negative

4. Automatic thoughts - Depressed people experience automatic thoughts that suggest inadequacy and hopelessness

Interpersonal Therapy = 4 problems that can lead to depression

1. Interpersonal Loss = losing someone important

Example: Someone becomes depressed after the death of a loved one or a painful breakup.

2. Interpersonal Role Dispute = dispute in role in relationship

Example: A couple argues about how much time to spend together or how to share chores; a worker disagrees with a boss about job duties.

3.Interpersonal Role Transition = Difficulty adjusting to a major life change that alters your social role or identity.

Example: Graduating, becoming a parent, retiring, losing a job, or moving to a new place.

4.Interpersonal Deficits = Problems forming or maintaining healthy relationships due to lack of social skills, trust, or support network.

Example: Someone has trouble making friends or tends to withdraw from others.
